std::vector<std::vector<double>> PropagateICs(std::vector<double> r, std::vector<double> v, double t0, double tf, double area, double reflectance, double mass, double drag_C, bool compute_drag, bool compute_srp, bool compute_third_body)

Returns propagated orbit solution given initial conditions.

The inputs of this function are given in an Earth centered inertial frame and returned in an Earth centered inertial frame. The output vectors are stored in a single vector such that each index corresponds to a single output vector. t=output[0], position (x,y,z) = output[1:3], velocity (u,v,w) = output[4:6], Hamiltonian = output[7].

Parameters

  • r – Initial position of satellite (km)

  • v – Initial velocity of satellite (km/s)

  • t0 – Epoch start time of satellite (s)

  • tf – Epoch end time of satellite (s)

  • area – “Cannonball” area of satellite (m^2)

  • reflectance – Coefficient of relectance of satellite

  • mass – Mass of satellite (kg)

  • drag_C – Drag coefficient of satellite

  • compute_drag – Boolean to toggle drag computation on or off

  • compute_SRP – Boolean to toggle solar radiation pressure computation on or off

  • compute_third_body – Boolean to toggle third body perturbation computation on or off

Returns

std::vector<std::vector<double> > The solutions stored in a vector of vectors {t,x,y,z,u,v,w,H}
